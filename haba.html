Haba pay

Haba na haba hujaza kibaba

The word Haba comes from the riddle or in Swahili language methali “Haba na haba hujaza kibaba” Haba was formed under analysis of mobile banking purpose in Kenya. It is an expansion of existing mobile banking features.It works within two main features, i.e on :Withdrawals and loans

HABA PAY FEATURES

1

Haba in cash withdrawal

With the existing mobile banking reach in the country haba has come to realize it can extend its reach through this service.Companies dealing with mobile banking e.g. safaricom,airtel,equity etc have mobile banking services based on cash withdrawal and cash deposits.Haba as a top up can expand this service futher.

How it works

Sometimes when a customer is withdrawing 490ksh from an agent and his balance is 518kshs the agent lacks change and has to ask the user for a certain small amount mostly less than 100 to complete a transaction Alternatively he goes to next shop to access some cash due to lack of change. This is where haba comes in where by it curbs lack of change by the agent hence haba fills this gap.It tops up the remaining amount to complete the transaction.It works on ussd and application format.

Charges

For haba top up on withdrawal the charge is 1ksh i.e.for every top up within the service. Based on this small charges a customer will find it more easier for him or her to pay for the top up. For the agent it saves for that transaction incase he did not have extra cash to concur to that transaction

2

Set off loans

How it works

Default loans have been on the increase within mobile banking loans. To be able to reduce these loans one needs a set off from a friend or family member to reduce his or her default rate.Every time you take a loan you are needed to pay on a specific date but what if you have no money at the specific time .Set off loans come in where by one can send a link requesting to be be paid for the existing loan and in return they can pay for them to avoid crb listing.

Charges

The charges are 1% off the normal charges.

3

Transport sector

Kenyas public transport system is key to the economy.It estimatesthat it contributes upto $3million a day.After a thorough review we noticed a gap in the public transport system and came up with a solution called ‘ Hab ma-3’.Ma-3 is a swahili word meaning matatu

How it works

The feature solves lack of change between the passenger and the tout just as it solves the problem in mobile banking within the withdrawal feature.Normally when a tout lacks change he would ask small amounts mostly in coin form to solve the problem.Secondly the tout move from bus to bus asking for change from other touts to be able to curb lack of change.We have put this small amounts into a digital haba wallet called piggybank, where one can save these amounts.We do believe we are in a digital economy hence creation of the ‘Ma-3’ feature.

When boarding a ‘Matatu’(Kenyas key public transport Carrier),we came to realize that touts who collect passengers fare had one problem.i.e. they lack change when transacting with the passenger,hence creation of the Ma-3 feature on haba App

It is accessible on App through QR. scan code
